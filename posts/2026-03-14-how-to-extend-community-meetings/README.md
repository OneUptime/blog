## Introduction

Open source projects thrive when community members contribute to improving processes and resources. Extending Cilium community meetings helps the entire Cilium community by making information more accessible and processes more efficient.

Contributing to community meetings does not require deep technical expertise -- clear communication, attention to detail, and understanding of community needs are equally valuable.

This guide covers how to contribute to and extend Cilium community meetings.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the Cilium project and its ecosystem
- Internet access and a calendar application
- Willingness to participate in community discussions

## Contributing to Community Meetings

### Proposing New Meeting Topics

1. Open the shared agenda document
2. Add your topic with your name and estimated time
3. Include any relevant links or context
4. Be prepared to lead the discussion

### Presenting at a Community Meeting

- Keep presentations focused and time-boxed
- Share slides or demos in advance when possible
- Leave time for questions
- Follow up on action items

### Improving Meeting Processes

Suggest improvements through:

- The #community Slack channel
- GitHub issues in the community repository
- Direct discussion during the meeting's open floor segment

### Starting a New Meeting Series

If you identify a need for a new regular meeting:

1. Discuss the idea in Slack and existing meetings
2. Create a proposal in the community repository
3. Define the meeting scope, schedule, and target audience
4. Set up calendar invites and shared documents
5. Announce through official channels

## Verification

Verify meeting links work, agendas are accessible, and recordings are available.

## Troubleshooting

- **Cannot find meeting links**: Check the Cilium community calendar and #community Slack channel.
- **Slack workspace access**: Request an invite through the Cilium website.
- **GitHub permissions**: Ensure your account has the necessary access for the repositories you need.
- **Timezone confusion**: All official times are in UTC. Use a timezone converter for your local time.

## Conclusion

Community meetings provide opportunities to engaging with the Cilium community. Active participation strengthens both your own Cilium practice and the broader community.
