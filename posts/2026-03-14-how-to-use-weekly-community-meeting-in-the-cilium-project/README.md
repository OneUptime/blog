## Introduction

Effective participation in open source projects requires understanding the available resources and processes. Cilium weekly community meetings provides essential information and collaboration opportunities for users and contributors alike.

Knowing how to navigate and weekly community meeting effectively helps you get the most out of the Cilium ecosystem, whether you are troubleshooting an issue, planning a deployment, or contributing code.

This guide covers practical steps for using Cilium weekly community meetings in your daily workflow.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the Cilium project and its ecosystem
- Internet access and a calendar application
- Willingness to participate in community discussions

## Joining the Weekly Community Meeting

### Meeting Details

The Cilium weekly community meeting takes place every Wednesday. Find current details at:

- **Cilium Community Calendar**: Published on the Cilium website
- **Slack #community channel**: Meeting links and reminders are posted
- **GitHub cilium/community**: Meeting schedule and agenda links

### Agenda and Preparation

```text
Typical Agenda:
1. Welcome and introductions
2. Release updates
3. Feature discussions
4. Community announcements
5. Open floor
```

Before the meeting:
- Review the shared agenda document
- Add any topics you want to discuss
- Prepare questions about ongoing development

### Participating Effectively

- Introduce yourself if it is your first time
- Keep comments focused and concise
- Use the chat for links and references
- Respect the facilitator's time management

### Accessing Recordings

Past meetings are recorded and available on the Cilium YouTube channel. Search for "Cilium Community Meeting" to find the playlist.

## Verification

Verify meeting links work, agendas are accessible, and recordings are available.

## Troubleshooting

- **Cannot find meeting links**: Check the Cilium community calendar and #community Slack channel.
- **Slack workspace access**: Request an invite through the Cilium website.
- **GitHub permissions**: Ensure your account has the necessary access for the repositories you need.
- **Timezone confusion**: All official times are in UTC. Use a timezone converter for your local time.

## Conclusion

Community meetings provide opportunities to engaging with the Cilium community. Active participation strengthens both your own Cilium practice and the broader community.
